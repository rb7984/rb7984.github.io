Riccardo Barelli - Computational Designer

I have experience in prefabrication and have applied my vision of Computational Design across various fields, including fabrication, 3D printing, exhibitions, and facade design. My work ranges from product design to architectural scale. Currently, I am coordinating a team at ZuruTech Milan to bring a revolutionary digital product to life.

For me, Computational Design is an approach to manage complexity in the design process. The chosen tools allow you to achieve excellent results, provided that an adequate method is designed.

The Hive at DigitalFutures in 2020

Workshop led by Gilles Retsin and Kevin Saey on the theme of Discrete Automation and Timber Housing. During the workshop it was discussed the concept of "Assemblage" and "Part": the discussion translated into the design of a single part system with defined sets of rules that let the architectural potential emerge from the relationships between the parts.

Master Degree Thesis at Unibo in 2021

Thesis aimed at exploring the architectural capabilities of a system designed for an automated construction process. The system consider a number of Parts and a set of Rules for their Interactions. Through an Assemblage process the Thesis explores the Emergent properties of the system.

"The Gold of the Tigers" at NIO Architecten, Rotterdam in 2022

Exploration project made in collaboration with NIO Architecten, to explore the plastic possibility of 3D printing on masks. The project consists of 10 masks with the relatives patterns to be applied. Grasshopper scripting has been used to distribute the pattern and to create gradients, following different strategies.
