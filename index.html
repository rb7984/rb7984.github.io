Riccardo Barelli - Computational Designer

I graduated in Architecture and Civil Engineering, in Bologna. During the time spent in university I’ve developed a passion for 3D Modeling and Visualization, using digital tools ranging from AutoCad to Rhino with Grasshopper, and exploring rendering techniques with Vray, Cinema 4D or 3D Studio Max. I experimented with fabrication and 3D printing in my curricular stage at WASP, exploring the potential of 3D printed housing processes. Here I’ve developed the design for Dior Pop-Up stores that has been exhibited at EXPO21 in Dubai. I also bring with me an additional knowledge in Computational Design developed writing my Master’s degree thesis: I’ve explored the architectural potential that a computational approach could express if we decide to apply it in the design process. I applied my vision of Computational design in various fields of work: from a design practice like the collaboration with Maurice Nio, to the Façade design with C41. Currently I'm experimenting something parallel that is the lead of a team to develop a digital product, at ZuruTech Milan. I always bring enthusiasm and precision in what I'm doing and I'm not scared to be put in uncomfortable places if it means I'm learning something new.
